SHORT HISTORY OF PASTRY IN FRANCE :

It is in the Middle Ages that the distinction between cooks and pastry cooks is made. Cakes lost their sacred character and became festive dishes. The blancmange, crêpes, flans and croissants date from this period. With the marriage of Catherine de Médicis to the future Henri II, the first ice creams appeared as well as the choux pastry invented by Popelini, a famous Italian pastry chef. In the 17th century, Anne of Austria brought back chocolate from the Spanish court, the gigantic buffets of the court at Versailles were adorned with "pièces montées" while Vatel invented the Chantilly cream in the castle of the same name. But it was in the 19th century that French pastry-making really took off. Antonin Carême published his book "le Pâtissier royal", which is still a reference. Meals end with millefeuilles, vacherins, savarins, profiteroles, éclairs and religieuses.
